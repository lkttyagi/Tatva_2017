ABOUT THE EVENT

  • Team size 3-5

Business plan is an idea which will be incorporated in future. This living document generally projects 3-5 years ahead and outlines the route a company intends to take to grow revenues. The participants are challenged to speak for five to eight minutes on their respective ideas. Presenters who will be presenting the business plan can be individuals or in a group. The business plan that the participants will be presenting would be consisting of the following components
Executive Summary The executive summary is a snapshot of the business plan as a whole and touches on your company profile and goals.
Company Description
The company description provides information on what to do, what differentiates your business from others, and the markets your business serves.
Market Analysis
Before launching any business, it is essential to research the business industry, market and competitors.
Organization & Management
Every business is structured differently. Find out the best organization and management structure for the business plan.
Service or Product Line
What do you sell? How does it benefit your customers? What is the product lifecycle? Get tips on how to tell the story about your product or service.
Marketing & Sales
How do you plan to market your business? What is your sales strategy? Read more about how to include this information in your plan.
Funding Request
If you are seeking funding for your business, find out about the necessary information you should include in your plan.
Financial Projections
If you need funding, providing financial projections to back up your request is critical.
Find out what information you need to include in your financial projections for your business plan.
Appendix
An appendix is optional, but a useful place to include information such as resumes, permits and leases. Find additional information you should include in your appendix.
How to Make Your Business Plan Stand Out
What makes your business unique? Determining this could help you stand out from the crowd and give you advantages over your competitors.
